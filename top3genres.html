Lofi music, a chill genre with jazz influences, features soft beats and smooth melodies creating a relaxing atmosphere. Popular for evoking nostalgia and calmness, it is favored for studying or unwinding. Listeners enjoy the cozy, tranquil vibe resembling a coffee shop or rainy day. Lofi's simplicity and seamless blend of elements offer a serene auditory journey, providing an escape from the constant stimuli of everyday life.

Rap beats are characterized by strong rhythmic patterns, often featuring heavy basslines and dynamic drum patterns to accompany rap vocals. Trap beats typically incorporate sharp hi-hats, booming 808 kicks, and intricate snare rolls, creating a dark and intense atmosphere for rap verses. Both styles emphasize groove and energy in their production.

Afrobeat beats are a fusion of traditional African rhythms with jazz, highlife, and funk. They feature complex percussion patterns, brass instruments, and call-and-response vocals. These beats draw inspiration from various African music styles, such as Nigerian highlife, Yoruba music, and Ghanaian palm-wine music, creating a vibrant and energetic sound.
